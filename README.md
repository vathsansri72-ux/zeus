The Hybrid Wheels project addresses the mobility gap in search-and-rescue and exploration robotics. 
By integrating foldable leg kinematics directly into a high-torque wheel assembly, this robot achieves high-speed traversal on flat ground and adaptive "stepping" capabilities for rugged terrain.

The Challenges;

Harsh Environments: Protecting delicate actuators from dust and impact.

Size Constraints: Fitting transformation linkages within a compact footprint.

Durability: Maintaining structural integrity during high-stress gait transitions.

1. Transformable Kinematics
The robot utilizes a unique four-bar linkage system (or specify your mechanism) that allows the legs to retract flush with the chassis.

2. Integrated Actuator Design
Primary Drive: Brushless DC (BLDC) motors for wheel rotation.

Transformation: High-torque micro-linear actuators (or servos) for leg deployment.

Material Selection: Designed for 7075 Aluminum or Carbon Fiber reinforced polymers to maximize the strength-to-weight ratio.

Sealed Joints: Actuators are housed within the frame to prevent debris ingress.

Shock Absorption: Passive suspension elements integrated into the wheel-leg interface.

Redundancy: The design allows for limited mobility even if one transformation actuator fails.
